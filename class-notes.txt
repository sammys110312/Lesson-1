1. Nucleus: Stores genetic material and coordinates cellular activities.
2. Mitochondria: Produces energy through cellular respiration.
3. Ribosomes: Synthesizes proteins.
4. Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER): Processes and transports proteins and lipids; rough ER has ribosomes, while smooth ER does not.
5. Golgi Apparatus: Modifies, sorts, and packages proteins and lipids for secretion or delivery to other organelles.
6. Lysosomes: Contains enzymes for digestion and waste removal.
7. Chloroplasts (in plant cells): Conducts photosynthesis to convert solar energy into chemical energy.
8. Cell Membrane: Protects the cell and regulates what enters and exits.
9. Cytoplasm: The gel-like substance where organelles are suspended and metabolic reactions occur.
